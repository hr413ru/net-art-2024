Beyond Western Borders

Curated by Hemy Rhee

Personally, I wanted to create an art exhibition that features a stronger focus on Asian artists because I feel like most net art I’ve seen was focused on European or American art. As an Asian myself, I was interested in how their net art will translate as they are living across the world. I looked forward to seeing what kinds of concepts and topics they discussed as well. I wanted to see their perspective on certain events that may be common to us, or how they incorporate their identity into their art. I found it interesting how the aesthetics were based off of Asian literature and art sometimes. I chose artworks that are more recent (after 2010), as they are more relevant to me.


unfold

Year: 2016

Artist: Ryoichi Kurokawa

Kurokawa collected data relating to the study of the formation of stars from the satellites of various space stations around the world to create a scene of nature. Mimicking traditional Japanese art that was centered around nature, Kurokawa’s work immerses its viewers with not only visual art, but also audio.


DOKU the Self

Year: 2022

Artist: Lu Yang

Lu Yang explores themes of identity, mortality, and spirituality through the lens of Buddhist philosophy and digital culture. Lu Yang does this by using game and pop culture visuals to create a video where he dances along music and different backgrounds.


moon

Year: 2013

Artist: Ai Weiwei and Olafur Eliasson

Weiwei and Eliasson use collaborative drawing to get people to draw onto a digital canvas. You’re able to draw anything you’d like, where you can non-verbally interact with others within their square of space.


a throw of the dice

Year: 2018

Artist: Akihiko Taniguchi

Taniguchi used a 3D scan of a landscape where a die actually fell. He 3D printed the object in a real-time simulation of the throwing of the dice.
